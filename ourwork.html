Our Work

We love to invests in knowledge, innovation, and solutions for advancing inclusive development.

PLF works with governments, companies, organizations and philanthropies in the pursuit of excellence in development services. Building on more than a decade of experience, we have deepened our expertise in several areas of focus. Our carefully developed approaches in areas such as research, data analytics, strategic advisory, institutions and governance, and designs – allow us to achieve our mission and increase our impact.

Knowledge Transfer

  • Critically engaging in research* to understand the knowledge gaps to facilitate consistent , innovative and adaptable solutions.
  • Creation/ Use of Knowledge.
  • Networking and knowledge sharing with policymakers, organizations, philanthropies, researchers, and communities.
  • Generating evidence, developing tools and resources

Driving Transformation

  • Shaping policies and programmes based on the people’s insights, experiences, views and aspirations.
  • Evolving and improving Policy Frameworks and Project Designs
  • Designing participatory, change management projects and effective use of disruptive change.
  • Transforming governance and management structures of government and global and national policy initiatives.

Strategic Advisory

  • Providing advisory and consultancy for large scale development projects of government, international agencies, donors, CSRs and CSOs.
  • Designing capacity building and support systems for strengthening governance and institution.
  • Effective use of disruptive change in bringing transformative change.
  • Extending support in strategic aspects such as vision building and project management.

Our History

A group of professionals with a vast experience in participatory and inclusive development initiatives have come together in the pursuit of excellence in development services in 2004. PLF was formally registered as a Trust and came into being under Indian law in 2014 and has been steadily evolving since. At the outset, PLF recognised the value that the efforts and endeavours of professionals acting outside the realm of government bring to the development practice.

2004

A group of senior professionals who worked in APRLP (a joint initiative of UK Aid and GoAP) came together as an informal group to continuously learn and contribute to pro poor and gender sensitive policy designs

2005-06

We responded to emerging needs in development by expanding mandate of the Foundation. Joined Danida’s Watershed Development Programme to document and distill lessons of watershed projects in India.

2007-09

Undertook Action Research and PMIAS in Micro-Finance, Livelihoods, Climate Change and National Flagship Programmes. Acted as ME&L Agency for AP Drought Adaptation Initiatives of World Bank and GoAP

2010-12

We provided support services to large-scale watershed projects of National Bank for Agriculture and Rural development (NABARD) in Andhra Pradesh

2012-14

Engaged in Action Research, PMIAS and Project Management Support activities to integrate concerns of the poor and vulnerable groups in different sectoral programmes

2015-18

Professionals from diverse knowledge fields joined the Think tank. We continued our efforts to innovate to emerge as a front-line institute to inspire & inform policy & practice on inclusive & sustainable development

2019

Keeping in view the changing times, our initiatives are aimed at reflecting on the learnings & investing on future policy priorities. Our Strategies include strengthening the knowledge ecosystem, providing advisories, support & improving policy frameworks.

Our Approach

  • Knowledge Synthesis

  • Finding Solutions
    • Policy formulation, Designing need based projects, Developing/Improving tools, Participatory approaches, Participatory Situation Analysis(PSA), Workbook for Participatory Action Planning, Cost Benefit Analysis (CBA) and Participatory Monitoring and Impact Assessment Systems (PMIAS)
  • Collective Engagement
    • Collaborating with government, international agencies, academicians, donors, CSRs and CSOs
  • Feeding learning into Policy

    • End to End Approach to provide solutions and Conducting pilots and action research to improve and shape new policy interventions

Thematic Area

Climate, Water and Natural Resources

For more than a decade we are associated in shaping policies related to climate, water and natural resources focusing on Watershed, Surface Irrigation and Ground Water Management. Our future actions are aimed at:

  • Strengthening institutions and infrastructure for better management of water and natural resources.
  • Facilitating policies and procedures for communities.
  • Providing sustainable solutions for climate resilience
  • Integrating climate change measures into policies, strategies and planning.

Poverty and Inclusion

We take a multidimensional view of poverty.For more than a decade, we have made efforts to integrate pro-poor development strategies in different sectoral programmes.In the coming years, we plan to shape policies and programmes to contribute towards:

  • Appropriating social protection systems for all.
  • Building resilience of poor.
  • Creating sound policy frameworks based on pro poor and gender sensitive development strategies.
  • Empowering of all women and girls at all levels.

Childhood and Youth

The well being of children and youth is a marker of a truly happy society.Policies and programmes are not fully equipped in responding to the needs and priorities of this segment of population.In shaping policies and programmes for children and youth we aim to contribute towards:

  • Reducing the proportion of youth not in employment, education or training
  • Operational zing a strategy for youth employment
  • Ensure healthy lives and promote nutrition an overall well being of children and youth
  • Ending discrimination and elimination of all forms of violence against all women and girls
  • Ensuring universal access to sexual and reproductive health and reproductive rights
  • Enhancing use of enabling technology such as information and communications.

Governance and Institutions

Bringing improvements in governance is a prerequisite for inclusive development, better quality of life and achieving sustainable economic growth. We believe this can be better achieved by building capabilities, promoting participation and ensuring responsiveness of all the actors. We support in:

  • Facilitating inclusive development to ensure that no one is left behind.
  • Strengthening public institutions
  • Government restructuring programmes
  • Developing impactful corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives.
  • Constructive engagement of civil society
  • Capacity building of various institutions and actors.
